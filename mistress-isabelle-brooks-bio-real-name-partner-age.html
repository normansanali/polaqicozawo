Fans are wishing to learn more about Isabelle. This article is about her real name, partner, age, and her career.

Mistress Isabelle Brooks On RuPaul’s Drag Race

Isabelle is one of the 16 contestants of RuPaul’s Drag Race. She’s a dancing diva with some serious disco fever, and there’s not a whole lot that can slow her down when she gets steam up. Isabelle describes her style as the top traditional drag with a modern twist. Glamorous ensembles and big, higher-the-hair-to-God wigs made her captivating.

Isabelle Brooks’s drag name is a play on her male name in addition to her drag mother’s last name with the addition of “mistress”. “Mistress” is a double entendre for “a bitch who is in control” and “a se*y sl*t”. “Isabelle” is the feminine version of her real name, Israel. “Brooks” comes for her drag mother, Chevelle Brooks.

She is the first queen from Houston to compete in the Drag Race franchise. However, she is already receiving abuse from “fans.”

“A lot of people think I’m a big girl and I’m fat but I’m really just big-boned,” she said. “But my heart goes out to the plus-size girls.” However, she clapped back at her haters on Twitter.

“I’ll never understand why people (especially queer ppl) are so OFFENDED by fat people being confident/happy,” Isabell wrote. “Just because you’re insecure and feel the need to tear yourself down doesn’t mean I do. I’m only fat because I’m a gluttonous bitch who likes to chow down.”

She added, “One of the most uncomfortable things for me is giving people access to my life as a boy. Not because I’m insecure/ feel some type of way, but bc I have worked hard to get to a place where I’m HAPPY. I love myself, and one day I hope you [cigarette emoji]s can learn to love yourself too.”

Mistress Isabelle Brooks Job

Mistress Isabelle Brooks started her drag career through So You Think You Can Drag in July 2016. A year later she posted on her IG, “A year ago today was my first time competing at SYTYCD! It feels like FOREVER ago… I’ve learned so much in this short period of time and I’m blessed to be able to call so many of you my sisters.”

But, it wasn’t easy for her to kick off her career. On her birthday in 2019, she posted on her Facebook about the problem that she had with her family. She stated, “When I was 16 I was shunned by my family, just for being who I was. Growing up, I never had a shoulder to lean on, or someone to tell me it was gonna be okay.”

She added, “There have been so many times in my life where I didn’t know if I could keep on going, but I had no other choice. Each moment in my life, good or bad, has been a learning experience and has shaped me into the person I am today. I found love and acceptance within my community and chosen family and discovered my passion and purpose in life through drag. Although life definitely has its ups and downs, I’m blessed every step of the way and so proud to be where I am today.” Isabelle won Miss Gay Louisiana USofA at Large 2019. According to her Facebook, she is a cast member at Hamburger Mary’s Houston and worked as a Showgirl at South Beach Houston. In September 2022, she has joined JR’s Houston as Monday Night Show Host. She is also known as “The Heavyweight Champ” the latest diva to enter the ring of Rupaul’s Drag Race Season 15.

Isabelle vowed to pass on the tradition of old-school drag to the younger generation because she thought it was losing out. She’s always been passionate about hair, long before drag, and had dreams of being a hairstylist. Because of this love, she started styling all of her own wigs.

Soon after, word of her jaw-dropping double and triple wig stacking spread all over the world. Her ability to style wigs for only herself and close friends to those of queens all over the world allowed her a chance to support herself financially until when the pandemic hit and caused the economy to drag down.

Soon, her wigs were being shipped to queens all around the globe, and she eventually founded her own wig business, Wigs By Mistress. also very wealthy. She once sold a double stack for $395.
