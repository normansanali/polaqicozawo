This cake is extra moist because we not only use ripe bananas but buttermilk as well to give it such a tender texture. I suggest using bananas that are very ripe and have brown spots on them. The bananas are definitely sweeter this way and are just right for banana bread or banana cake. 

The chocolate Nutella frosting is super simple. It is made with sweet cream butter, Nutella, powdered sugar, and a touch of cream.  If you want some extra Nutella flavor, I suggest drizzling a little Nutella all over the frosted cake.

How to make Banana Chocolate Chip Cake:

  • Use Ripe Bananas.  You want to use over-ripe bananas with black spots. You want to see those black spots on the yellow peel, because that is all flavor. It brings so much natural sweetness to the cake. If your bananas arenâ€™t quite ripe enough, place in a brown paper bag and place in a dark place.
  • Sprinkle fresh lemon juice over mashed bananas. It helps bring some acidity to the cake and keep the bananas from turning brown.
  • Use Buttermilk. It is a true miracle worker in baked goods. It helps to create such a tender texture.  The beauty of it is that it has the same amount of fat as 1% milk yet is so thick and creamy.
  • Use Mini Chocolate Chips, if possible. This way you get chocolate chips in every bite. I use the Nestle mini semi-sweet chocolate chips. 

I use three8-inch cake pans for this recipe. You can also use two 9-inch cake pans but the layers will just be a tad thicker.

Chocolate Nutella Frosting:

  • Preheat oven to 350 degrees. In a large mixing bowl, cream butter, sugar, and brown sugar for 5 minutes or until light and fluffy.

  • Add eggs one at a time, mixing in between. Stir in vanilla. In small bowl, mash bananas with lemon juice. Add bananas to bowl and mix well. Stir in buttermilk.

  • Add flour, baking soda, baking powder, and salt. Fold in mini-chocolate chips. Pour into greased baking pans. Use your favorite non-stick cooking spray or butter and then a light dusting of flour. I suggest using three 8-inch cake pans or two 9-inch cake pans.

  • Bake for 22-26 minutes or until the center of the cake is completely baked.

  • Let cool and then remove from pans.

  • In a large mixing bowl, cream together butter and nutella. Add powdered sugar and whip until light and fluffy. Add cream as needed.

  • Frost cooled layers with Nutella frosting. If you want more Nutella flavoring, drizzle some all over the top of the cake or sprinkle with chocolate shavings.
