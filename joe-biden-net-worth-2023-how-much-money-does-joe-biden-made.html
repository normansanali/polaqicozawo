Joe Biden Net Worth 2023: How Much Money Does Joe Biden Made?

July 2024 · 3 minute read

The most powerful leader of the free world, President Joe Biden, isn’t just making waves in politics. As of recent estimates, his real estate investments have seen a substantial appreciation, taking his net worth to an impressive $10 million, up from $8 million at the onset of his presidency.

Biden’s Real Estate Goldmine

Unlike many high-ranking officials, Biden’s fortune isn’t amassed through complex business dealings across continents. Instead, the savvy politician is reaping rewards from a source familiar to many Americans: real estate.

Rehoboth Beach Property: An Astute Investment

Biden’s priciest possession is a 4,800-square-foot summer haven in Rehoboth Beach, acquired in 2017 post his vice-presidential tenure. This $4.5 million jewel was initially perceived as an extravagant purchase, especially when he added an estimated $75,000 pool. However, as homebuyers scrambled for spacious properties amidst the pandemic, this house’s valuation skyrocketed, offering Biden a $1.7 million appreciation over its acquisition price.

Wilmington Estate: Biden’s Personal Haven

His longstanding connection with Wilmington is epitomized by his estate in the Greenville neighborhood. Initially a bare piece of land bought in 1996, Biden later adorned it with a sprawling 6,850-square-foot colonial residence, further adding a 1,900-square-foot cottage. Today, this property stands valued at $2.5 million, experiencing a $700,000 appreciation in just two years.

Together, these homes make up nearly 70% of Biden’s fortune.

Middle-Class Joe: A Glimpse into His Affinities

Dubbed “Middle-Class Joe,” Biden’s penchant for luxury isn’t hidden. His 1967 Corvette Stingray, a wedding gift from his father, remains a testament to his tastes. This classic car, initially costing around $5,600 (equivalent to $51,000 today), currently boasts a $100,000 price tag.

Biden’s Historical Real Estate Adventures

Biden’s real estate journey traces back to his mid-twenties. Purchasing properties in Maryland, he aspired to create a family compound. Despite financial constraints, he continued his investment spree, acquiring a 10,000-square-foot mansion in 1975, previously owned by the esteemed DuPonts of Delaware. This mansion, bought amidst considerable debt, was sold for a whopping $1.2 million in 1996.

Presidential Assets and Earnings

Like many senior citizens, the crux of Biden’s wealth lies in his homes. Throughout the years, Biden has refinanced his mortgages to ensure liquidity.

Financial relief came post his vice-presidential tenure, with Biden amassing earnings of over $17 million between 2017 and 2020. This influx helped his net worth surge from an estimated $2.5 million to $8 million.

Today, President Biden’s portfolio outshines inflation, majorly thanks to his real estate investments. He remains a minimal stock market player, with less than $310,000 invested in mutual funds, and the rest nestled in bank accounts. Besides, his presidential salary and pension cumulatively add around $650,000 to his annual earnings.

Future Financial Prospects

While President Biden has ample cash reserves, he retains the option to leverage his real estate holdings, evident from the home-equity line of credit he recently secured against his Rehoboth property. However, certain assets, like his cherished Corvette, remain priceless. As he playfully mentioned, selling it might just invoke a heavenly intervention from his late son, Beau.

President Joe Biden’s evolving financial landscape serves as a testament to the potential of real estate investment. In a world of fluctuating stocks and unpredictable business ventures, Biden’s story underlines the age-old wisdom: there’s no place like home, especially when it appreciates in value!
