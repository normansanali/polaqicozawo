How Much is Parking at Fisherman's Wharf in San Francisco?

September 2024 · 7 minute read

Fisherman’s Wharf is one of the most popular tourist destinations in San Francisco, drawing millions of visitors each year. With limited street parking, where you park and how much you pay is an important consideration when visiting the area.

If you’re short on time, here’s the quick answer: Parking rates at Fisherman’s Wharf range from $3 per hour (street parking) up to $50 per day for garage parking on peak days. Parking can be easiest at Pier 39’s garages but is often most affordable a few blocks away.

Parking Options at Fisherman’s Wharf

When visiting Fisherman’s Wharf in San Francisco, finding parking can be a challenge. However, there are several options available for those looking to park their vehicles.

Street Parking

Street parking is one option for parking near Fisherman’s Wharf. There are metered parking spaces available on some streets surrounding the area. Prices for street parking can vary depending on the time of day and the specific location.

It is important to pay attention to parking signs and regulations to avoid any parking tickets.

It’s worth noting that street parking can be limited and hard to find during peak tourist seasons or busy weekends. Therefore, it is recommended to arrive early or consider alternative parking options.

Parking Lots

Another option for parking at Fisherman’s Wharf is utilizing parking lots. There are a few parking lots in the vicinity that offer hourly or daily rates. These lots provide convenient access to the attractions at the wharf.

Prices for parking lots can vary depending on the duration of your stay. It is a good idea to check the rates in advance and compare them to other parking options to find the best deal. Some parking lots may offer discounts for longer stays or special promotions, so it’s worth doing some research.

Parking Garages

Parking garages are another option for parking near Fisherman’s Wharf. There are several parking garages in the area that provide secure and covered parking spaces. These garages typically have hourly and daily rates.

One advantage of parking garages is that they offer protection from the weather and provide peace of mind for those concerned about the safety of their vehicles. Additionally, some parking garages may offer valet parking services for added convenience.

It’s important to note that parking garages tend to be more expensive compared to street parking or parking lots. However, they can be a good option if you prefer a secure and covered parking space.

For more information on parking options at Fisherman’s Wharf, you can visit the official website of the San Francisco Municipal Transportation Agency at www.sfmta.com.

Fisherman’s Wharf Parking Rates

When visiting Fisherman’s Wharf in San Francisco, it’s important to plan ahead for parking. The area is known for its popular attractions, delicious seafood, and stunning waterfront views, so parking can sometimes be a challenge.

Here is some information about the parking rates at Fisherman’s Wharf to help you prepare for your visit.

Hourly, Daily, and Monthly Rates

Fisherman’s Wharf offers both hourly and daily parking options, making it convenient for visitors who want to explore the area for a few hours or spend the entire day. The hourly rates typically range from $5 to $10, depending on the parking lot or garage you choose.

For those planning to stay longer, daily rates are available and usually range from $25 to $40. Some parking facilities also offer monthly rates for those who frequent the area or work nearby.

It’s important to note that parking rates may vary depending on the time of year and demand. During peak tourist seasons, such as summer or holidays, prices may be slightly higher. It’s always a good idea to check the current rates and availability before your visit to avoid any surprises.

Special Event and Peak Pricing

During special events or peak hours, such as major holidays or weekends, parking rates at Fisherman’s Wharf may be subject to change. This is done to manage the increased demand and ensure that there is enough parking available for visitors. It’s not uncommon to see higher rates during these times.

If you’re planning to visit Fisherman’s Wharf during a special event or peak season, it’s advisable to arrive early or consider alternative transportation options such as public transit or ridesharing services.

This can help you avoid the hassle of searching for parking and potentially save you money.

For the most up-to-date information on parking rates at Fisherman’s Wharf, it’s recommended to visit the official website of the parking facility you plan to use. Websites like www.fishermanswharf.org provide detailed information on rates, availability, and any special promotions or discounts that may be available.

Tips for Finding Affordable Parking

Arrive Early to Get Street Parking

One of the best ways to find affordable parking near Fisherman’s Wharf in San Francisco is to arrive early. By getting there before the crowds, you increase your chances of finding street parking at a reasonable price.

Street parking can be a great option if you are willing to search for a spot and don’t mind walking a few blocks to your destination. Keep in mind that parking regulations and availability can vary, so it’s a good idea to check the city’s website for up-to-date information on parking restrictions and rates.

Park Further Away and Walk

If you’re looking to save even more money on parking, consider parking further away from Fisherman’s Wharf and walking to your destination. There are several parking lots and garages located a few blocks away from the waterfront that offer more affordable rates compared to the lots right near the Wharf.

While it may require a bit more time and effort, parking further away and taking a leisurely stroll can be a great way to save some extra cash and enjoy the beautiful surroundings of San Francisco.

Consider Public Transportation

Another option to consider when looking for affordable parking at Fisherman’s Wharf is to take advantage of public transportation. San Francisco has an extensive public transportation system, including buses, trains, and cable cars, that can easily get you to and from the Wharf.

Not only will you save money on parking fees, but you’ll also avoid the hassle of finding a parking spot in a busy area. Plus, public transportation allows you to sit back, relax, and enjoy the sights of the city without the stress of driving and parking.

For more information on parking options and rates near Fisherman’s Wharf, you can visit the official website of the San Francisco Municipal Transportation Agency (https://www.sfmta.com/). They provide detailed information on parking garages, street parking regulations, and public transportation routes to help you make the best decision for your visit to Fisherman’s Wharf.

Additional Parking Considerations

Parking Validation

When visiting Fisherman’s Wharf in San Francisco, it is important to consider parking validation options. Some businesses in the area offer parking validation, which can help offset the cost of parking.

This means that if you make a purchase or use a service at a participating establishment, you may be eligible for discounted or even free parking. It’s a great way to save some money while enjoying the attractions and amenities of Fisherman’s Wharf.

ADA Accessible Parking

If you require ADA accessible parking, Fisherman’s Wharf provides designated parking spaces for individuals with disabilities. These spaces are located in convenient areas to ensure easy access to the attractions and facilities.

It is important to display a valid disabled parking permit or license plate when using these spaces. Additionally, it’s a good idea to check the specific parking regulations and requirements for ADA accessible parking in San Francisco to ensure a smooth experience.

For more information about parking at Fisherman’s Wharf, you can visit the official website of the San Francisco Municipal Transportation Agency (SFMTA) at www.sfmta.com. The website provides up-to-date information on parking rates, availability, and any changes or updates to the parking policies in the area.

Conclusion

With street parking at $3 per hour and garage parking as high as $50 per day, the cost of parking at Fisherman’s Wharf can quickly add up. By arriving early, parking a few blocks away, and taking advantage of validations, you can find more affordable rates during your visit.
