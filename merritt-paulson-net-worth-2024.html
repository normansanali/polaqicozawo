Who is Merritt Paulson?

Merritt Paulson is an American businessman and sports team owner. He is most notably recognized for his ownership of the Portland Timbers, a Major League Soccer (MLS) team, and the Portland Thorns FC, a National Women’s Soccer League (NWSL) team. His involvement in sports extends beyond ownership, as he plays an active role in the management and strategic direction of his teams.

Early Life and Education

Paulson was born into a family with a strong financial background. His father, Henry Paulson, is a former United States Secretary of the Treasury. Merritt Paulson attended Harvard University, where he earned his undergraduate degree, and later received his MBA from Harvard Business School, setting the stage for a successful career in business and sports management.

Business Ventures and Investments

Before venturing into sports team ownership, Paulson worked in various business roles. His early career included positions at HBO and the National Basketball Association (NBA), where he gained valuable experience in the sports entertainment industry. He later founded Shortstop, LLC, the entity through which he owns his sports teams.
