How did Dwight Howard start his Professional Career?

Dwight Howard was selected by the Orlando Magic in 2004 as the first overall pick in the NBA draft. If he hadn’t been dealt to the Brooklyn Nets, he would have been a free agent at the end of his final campaign with the Orlando Magic. He was traded to the Los Angeles Lakers on August 10, 2012. At that time, Chris Duhon and Earl Clark were also acquired by the Los Angeles Lakers.

Howard participated in games for the Atlanta Hawks, Los Angeles Lakers, Dallas Mavericks, Houston Rockets, and Golden State Warriors in the first week of July. He did, nevertheless, aim to sign with the Rockets and did so on July 13, 2013.

On July 12, 2016, Howard and the Atlanta Hawks reached a three-year, $70 million contract agreement. Following Tim Duncan’s retirement, he started the 2016–2017 season as the NBA’s active leader in rebounds and blocked shots. He made his debut during the inaugural season.

On June 20, 2017, the Charlotte Hornets acquired Howard in exchange for Miles Plumlee and Marco Belinelli. The Brooklyn Nets acquired him in exchange for Timofey Mozgov, but they released him right away.
