Sophie Winkleman And Her Husband Had A Grand Royal Wedding

August 2024 · 5 minute read

English actress Sophie Winkleman and her husband Lord Frederick Windsor tied the knot in a grand ceremony over a decade ago.

Winkleman and Windsor initially crossed paths during a night out in Soho. Gradually, their connection blossomed into a romantic relationship. After spending a considerable amount of time together, the duo exchanged vows on September 12, 2009.

For their wedding venue, the couple chose the historic Hampton Court Palace, a Grade I listed royal residence in the London Borough of Richmond upon Thames, favored by Henry VIII.

On that memorable day, the grounds of Hampton Court Palace were aglow with candle lanterns, casting a warm apricot hue on the spectacular fountain at the great hall’s entrance.

Likewise, approximately 400 guests graced the wedding, including notable figures such as Princess Eugenie, the Duke and Duchess of Kent, Lady Annabel Goldsmith, actress Jane Asher, Lady Helen Taylor, and singer Bryan Adams.

Moreover, the actress wore a stunning £5,000 silk gown designed by Anna Bystrova from the Russian couture house Roza. Bystrova, a favorite designer of the bride’s mother-in-law.

Sophie Winkleman and Lord Frederick Windsor have been happily married for 14 years. (Source: Hello)

Following the nuptials, Princess Michael of Kent openly expressed her sentiments about her relationship with Winkleman, stating, “Already she is like a daughter to us. We are enchanted with Sophie.”

Similarly, in an interview with The Times, the actress shared her delightful experiences since becoming a member of the Royal Family.

Reflecting on the warm reception, she explained, “I’ve been incredibly welcomed with open arms by all of them. The Queen’s been wonderful, Prince Charles has been wonderful, Prince William’s been heaven.”

Winkleman candidly shared insights into the compromises she navigates while balancing her career and family life with Windsor.

Reflecting on her experiences, she revealed, “After Two and a Half Men, I received some fantastic job offers in America, but I chose not to pursue them because I couldn’t fathom simply disappearing to another country for months.”

She added, “When you have a family, there are inevitable compromises you make in your career. Consequently, I opt for jobs in Britain, allowing me to be with them every week.”

Although Winkleman and Windsor are not active on social media, they are occasionally seen together at public events. For instance, in June 2023, the couple enjoyed a rare night out in central London, joining fellow royal Lady Amelia Windsor at an event.

The actress seemed to be in particularly high spirits as they left the venue. She kissed her husband before hopping into a taxi with a friend, while Windsor headed off to a different event.

Sophie Winkleman’s husband, Lord Frederick Windsor, is the son of Prince and Princess Michael of Kent

Windsor was born on April 6, 1979, at St Mary’s Hospital, London, and was christened on July 11, 1979, at the Chapel Royal, St James’s Palace, Westminster.

Growing up at Kensington Palace alongside his cousins Prince Harry and Prince William, he received his education at Wetherby School, Sunningdale, and Eton College, where he excelled as an Oppidan Scholar.

After completing his studies at Magdalen College, Oxford, earning a 2:1 degree in Classics, Windsor embarked on a diverse career journey. Initially, he ventured into fashion modeling, working with Burberry and designer Tomasz Starzewski.

He also explored music journalism, notably contributing to Tatler magazine. Despite initially planning to become an entertainment law solicitor, he transitioned to working as an analyst in the wealth management arm of JP Morgan in London, as reported by The Times.

Currently, Windsor is an accomplished banker and executive director at JPMorgan Chase. Notably, he holds the title of a CFA charter holder, having received his charter in 2013.

Sophie Winkleman and Lord Frederick Windsor are proud parents of two children

Winkleman and Windsor joyfully welcomed their first child, daughter Maud Elizabeth Daphne Marina, in August 2013.

Explaining the choice of their daughter’s name, Windsor shared, “We liked the idea of an old-fashioned English name, and there are a couple of Princess Mauds in my father’s family from a hundred years ago.”

The couple welcomed their second child, a daughter named Isabella Alexandra May, on January 16, 2017. An official statement from the monarchy confirmed that the baby was safely delivered at 11:59 p.m. at Chelsea and Westminster Hospital.

Sophie Winkleman beamed as she carried her younger daughter, Isabella Alexandra May. (Source: DailyMail)

The actress and her husband intentionally maintain a low profile for their children, Maud and Isabella, steering them away from the public spotlight. Both girls attended the exclusive £20,000-a-year Thomas’s Battersea.

In a candid interview with The Times, Winkleman voiced her concerns regarding the influence of digital devices on children’s learning.

Her worries extended beyond the potential hazards of unrestricted internet access, as she described the online landscape as a “toxic wilderness” lacking sufficient protection for children.

Expressing specific unease about the accessibility of extremist content, she underscored her dedication to managing her elder daughter’s tablet usage strictly, confining it to a specific time frame on Sundays.

In alignment with her concerns, the actress also voiced support for a parent group advocating for the prohibition of smartphones for those under 16.

Brief on Sophie Winkleman’s career

Winkleman’s journey as an actress commenced with her involvement in the Cambridge University Footlights Dramatic Club, where she began writing and performing for the group.

Following her time at the Cambridge Footlights, she further honed her skills at the National Youth Theatre, participating in numerous acts.

Transitioning to the stage, she spent a season at the Royal Shakespeare Company, collaborating on various well-received plays, notably under the direction of the legendary Sir Peter Reginald Frederick Hall.

While Winkleman has made notable contributions to the theater, her television career took off in the late 1990s. Her inaugural credited appearance on the small screen was as the character Joely in the miniseries White Teeth.

She later garnered praise for her performances in two episodes of the show Waking the Dead. Moving into the 2000s, she graced television screens in series like Keen Eddie, Poirot, and Dalziel and Pascoe before landing the significant role of Princess Eleanor in the miniseries The Palace.

In addition to her television endeavors, Winkleman has also left her mark in the world of film. Some of her notable movies include Natalie Encore, Love Live Long, and many more.
