Michael Buffer is American professional wrestling, ring announcer and National Football League also known for his trademarked catchphrase: ” Let’s get ready to rumble”

Michael Buffer was born in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania on November 2, 1944. His father was enlisted in the United States Navy during World War II.

When he was 11 months of age, his parents got divorced, and he was then raised by a foster parent, who was a school bus driver and housewife in Roslyn, Pennsylvania.

The early life of Michael Buffer

Michael Buffer was born and raised in Pennsylvania where he was born in a different family but raised by foster parents. He is the son of an enlisted man in the United States Navy and his wife during World War II.

At the age of 20, he was enlisted in United States Army during the Vietnam War and he served till the age of 23.

Michael even had various jobs including a car salesman, and at the age of 32, he began a modeling career before becoming a ring announcer at the age of 38.

Michael Buffer Biography

Buffer began his career as a ring announcer in 1982. By 1983, he was given national identity as he was announcing all boxing matches promoted by Bob Arum’s Top Rank on ESPN, at a time when ring announcers were strictly locally hired talent.

He was the exclusive ring announcer for all bouts in Donald Trump-owner casinos in the 1980s. Many boxers admired his work.

Until 2001, he was the ring announcer for World Championship Wrestling main events featuring Hulk Hogan or other Top WCW talent. 

Buffer signed with DAZN in 2018, to serve as a ring announcer for all of its boxing broadcasts, which also include deals with Matchroom and Canelo Alvarez.
